Back in the bad old days of the Dot.com boom in San Francisco, I worked for SquareTrade, which at that time was doing dispute resolution for eBay buyers and sellers.

My job mainly consisted of saying no - a lot. Mostly to sellers who wanted negative feedback removed from their profiles. At that time there was no procedure to have feedback taken off, usually all we could do was put a blurb on the seller's profile saying that they had tried to resolve the issue.

To say that the majority of the people I worked with were upset would be putting it mildly. For the most part, they were honest people who worked very hard to create their business only to have it hurt by what they saw as an unjustified attack.

Unfortunately, there was little I could do for most of them, except listen. But turns out that listening helps, it really does. Commiserating, feeling like you're being heard and not just sending emails off into a void...it all helps. That job really taught me that you deal with people, not problems. Sometimes you can't resolve a problem, but if you deal with the people decently, it may be enough.
